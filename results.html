Understanding Your Results

If a result is marked as abnormal or borderline, your GP will contact you to discuss the next steps. If your results are normal and no action is needed, you may not be contacted directly.

Results typically take 3-5 working days from the date of your blood test. Some specialist tests may take longer.

NHS Screening Programmes

The NHS offers free screening programmes to help detect conditions early:

  • Cervical screening: Women aged 25-64, every 3-5 years
  • Breast screening: Women aged 50-71, every 3 years
  • Bowel cancer screening: Everyone aged 60-74, every 2 years
  • Abdominal aortic aneurysm: Men at age 65
  • NHS Health Check: Ages 40-74, every 5 years
